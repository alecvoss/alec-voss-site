The Guadalupe River
Flood Safety Guide

139 people died on July 4th, 2025. One year later, the system built around this river has barely changed. Know this before you go.

Why this river is different
Limestone does not absorb rain. It sheds it.
The Edwards Plateau — 24,000 square miles of impermeable rock — cannot hold rainfall. Water reaches the river corridor within hours of a storm, not days.
1,900 square miles drain into one corridor
Every drop of rain anywhere in the watershed — even rain you cannot see — passes through the same river. Storm upstream, flood downstream.
26 feet in 45 minutes
On July 4th, 2025 the river rose the equivalent of five swimming pools stacked on top of each other in less time than a television episode. 750 girls were sleeping in cabins on the bank.

Before you ever get in the water
The 48-hour rule
Do not enter the Guadalupe within 48 hours of significant rainfall anywhere in the watershed, not just where you are standing. Rain near Hunt, Junction, or Comfort is already moving toward you even on a clear day downriver.
Check 01
USGS gauge at Sattler — Station 08167500
Normal: 200 to 400 CFS. Above 800, experienced guides change plans. Above 1,500, do not enter.
Check 02
Canyon Lake pool elevation
Army Corps releases change current speed downstream with no local warning at the put-in.
Check 03
Upstream weather — not local
Check Hunt and Junction, not New Braunfels. Clear skies above you means nothing about what is moving toward you from upstream.
Check 04
Do not rely on CodeRED alone
Opt-in only. Shown to deliver alerts hours late on July 4th. Check the gauge yourself.
If you are sending a child to camp on this river
Ask whether there is an automatic flood alert system
Not a phone tree. Not a calling chain. An automatic system that triggers without a human making a decision. Ask for it in writing. The Camp Mystic phone tree was the primary warning system on the night 27 people died there.
Ask for the written nighttime evacuation plan
Specifically ask: what happens if the river rises while campers are sleeping? A piece of paper saying "stay in your cabin until given instructions" is not an evacuation plan. That was the Camp Mystic protocol on July 4th.
Identify high ground before nightfall
Know the nearest high ground from any cabin or tent site before dark. Not when water is at the door — before anyone goes to sleep.
Bring a real Type III PFD for tubing
The foam disc on a tube rental does not keep an unconscious person face-up. A Coast Guard rated NRS Ninja or Stohlquist equivalent does. Bring your own.
What still has not changed — one year later
Status 01
No county siren system
The $1M warning system was declined because sirens would disturb the peace. Still not installed.
Status 02
Camps reopened in the flood plain
FEMA removed buildings from the official hazard designation after appeals. The river did not move with the maps.
Status 03
IPAWS still not default-activated
The technology to alarm every phone in the valley remains available, unused, exactly as it was on July 4th.
Status 04
NWS staffing gaps persist
Warning coordination roles in the affected forecast offices remain a known vacancy in the regional system.
